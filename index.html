At the center of Piazza San Pietro stands an ancient Egyptian
Obelisk, which was brought to Rome in 37 AD and erected in
its current location in 1586.
The obelisk is a towering 40 meters high and serves as a focal
point of the square, symbolizing the triumph of Christianity
over paganism.
Theodor Ameyden said that the jet of water from the top of the fountain
“seemed to rise in the air like a veritable river.”
He called it “the most beautiful fountain which exists in Europe”
The fountain had no pumps and operated purely by gravity, with a source of water higher
than the fountain which caused the water to shoot upwards.
In 1612, Pope Paul V commissioned Carlo Maderno to redesign the fountain on St. Peter’s Square,
originally built in 1490 during the time of Pope Innocent VIII
After the completion of the Maderno Fountain,
Pope Clement X commissioned Pietro Bernini
to build the second fountain on the Southern
side of the Obelisk. The fountain was
completed in 1677.
The baldacchino, Bernini’s first work at St. Peter’s, stands below the dome.
At 28.7 meter tall (94.3 ft), it is the largest piece of bronze in the world.
